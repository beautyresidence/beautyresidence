What is a nucleotide?

A nucleotide is the basic building block of nucleic acid polymers (e.g. DNA = deoxyribonucleic acid, RNA = ribonucleic acid). The linear sequence of nucleotides contains all information for structure, development and function of an organism.

Indications

Juvenus should be used as a temporary filler for the correction of small skin defects.  The different concentration of polynucleotides in the two preparations allows the treatment of different types of spots.  "Juvenus" can be used on various parts of the body: neck, décolleté, face (periocular area, cheeks, cheekbones), back of the hands, abdomen, thighs and glutes.


Dosage

Dosage depends on the skin condition of the patient. Do not exceed the quantity of one syringe (2 mL) per site and per session. The amount of product to be injected can be easily controlled through the graduated syringe.


Contraindications

Juvenus must not be used:

*for peri-orbital injections (eyelids, sockets); *for injections into blood or lymph vessels.

Juvenus must not be administered to patients with:

*circulatory disorders; *ongoing dermatitis; *epilepsy; *diabetes; *ongoing herpes infection; *infection; *previous or ongoing autoimmune diseases or to subjects undergoing immunotherapy; *serious allergies or ongoing inflammation; *acknowledged hypersensitivity to the product components.

Juvenus must not be used on pregnant or breastfeeding women.

Juvenus must not be used on people below the age of eighteen.
